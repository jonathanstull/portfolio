About Me

Since I graduated from college, I have explored the world of writing in numerous ways. As a journalist, I learned how to communicate with people and translate their stories for print and web audiences. I have adapted this skill to successfully build brand narratives and web copy, write fiction, document the outdoors, and represent nonprofits via grant strategy.

Writing is hard. In 2020, following periods of pandemic-related unemployment, I realized that I had found a ceiling in my writing career, and I jumped into action in three important ways:

  1. I joined WVDO, a nonprofit grantwriter's workshop to build new skills
  2. I joined a Portland-based economic justice nonprofit, Project LEDO, to facilitate its grant strategy
  3. I enrolled in Epicodus, a coding bootcamp, to build skills that will open new opportunities and challenges

Over the years, I have developed a loyal ethic and a commitment to creating a better world through the work that I do. I'm excited to apply new skills and experience to climate-conscientious tech startups.

If you are interested in learning more about who I am and what I believe, you might be interested to read my personal essay, What 'The End of the Myth' Means for Nature and the American Dream.
